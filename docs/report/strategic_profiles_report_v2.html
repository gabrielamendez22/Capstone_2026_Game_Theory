01 · What Changed from v1

Revisions Implemented

Four methodological corrections were applied following supervisor feedback:

θ — Exploitation intensity (corrected). Previously computed as extraction / fixed sustainable_share. Now computed as extractioni,t / (pool_before_regent × regen_rate / num_players), making the benchmark pool-relative and time-varying. Sustainable play = 1.0. regen_rate is inferred from the sustainable_share column (stored as integer percentage × 10; e.g. 2 → 0.20). θ values dropped from ~4.5 to ~2.0, which is consistent with the actual extraction behaviour observed in the data.
η — Split by game condition. Pooled η replaced by η_aligned (honesty when sender incentive = cooperate) and η_misaligned (honesty when sender incentive = deceive). Primary estimate is Δη = η_aligned − η_misaligned, the honesty drop under misalignment. Each run_id is entirely aligned or misaligned; Δη is computed by averaging across runs within the same file × matchup × model group before aggregating.
γ — Misaligned rounds only. Previously computed on all rounds. Now γ_mis = Pearson r(message_truthful, action_correct) restricted to misaligned runs, where the sender has a genuine incentive to deceive. Pooled γ is retained in the game-level file for reference.
β — No longer pooled across environments. β_pd, β_cd, β_ct are reported separately. Cross-environment coherence of β is now measured as Pearson correlations among per-model β means (r(β_PD–CD), r(β_PD–CT), r(β_CD–CT)) rather than variance of a pooled mean.

04 · Key Finding

Δη — Strategic Deception Under Misalignment

Δη measures how much a model lowers its honesty when it has an incentive to deceive. Four of six models exhibit substantial strategic adaptation (~0.43–0.52). Two models show near-zero adaptation, suggesting they do not adjust their behaviour to the strategic context.

GPT-4o-mini and Gemini Flash Lite appear to lack the context-sensitivity to adjust honesty to incentive structure — this is a relevant finding for H2 (instruction tuning) and potentially for deployment contexts where strategic communication is expected.

06 · Coherence

β Cross-Environment Correlations

Belief calibration is now reported separately per environment and coherence is measured as cross-model correlations of per-environment β means.

PairrInterpretation
r(β_PD, β_CT)+0.909 Strong positive correlation — models that calibrate well in PD also calibrate well in CT. Both involve binary cooperation/following decisions.
r(β_PD, β_CD)−0.365 Weak negative — CD belief calibration is not predicted by PD performance. Consistent with CD measuring a structurally different belief domain.
r(β_CD, β_CT)−0.473 Weak negative — further support that CD belief calibration is distinct. CD beliefs concern resource extraction levels, not binary actions.
Note: The β_PD–CT correlation (r = 0.909) suggests a shared calibration ability across binary-action games. β_CD operates on a different domain (continuous extraction, binary threshold) and should not be averaged with the other two. This validates Carlos's concern about cross-environment β poolability.

Key structural finding: The Δη–γ_mis correlation (r = −0.608) is the strongest pairwise relationship. Models that adapt their honesty strategically under misalignment (high Δη) tend to be less influenced by sender messages as receivers (low γ_mis). This suggests a coherent "strategic awareness" dimension spanning both CT roles: models that know when to deceive also know when not to be deceived. With n = 6 this does not reach conventional significance, but the direction and magnitude are theoretically meaningful.
08 · Model Patterns

Notable Observations

GPT-4o
Human-like ρ (0.450, p=0.27). Lowest θ in the frontier tier (1.83). Strong strategic adaptation (Δη=0.44). Best β_CD (0.409). Most balanced profile across all four parameters.
Claude Sonnet
Highest strategic deception (Δη=0.519 — drops from 98% to 46% honest). Significantly less reciprocal than humans (ρ=0.091, p<0.0001). Despite being a smaller model than Opus, shows stronger CT strategic adaptation.
Gemini 2.5 Flash
Near-zero reciprocity (ρ=0.009) — plays as if opponent behaviour is irrelevant. Highest exploitation (θ=2.39). Good β_CT (0.283). The most "context-blind" model in PD, yet strategically adaptive in CT (Δη=0.43).
GPT-4o-mini
Δη≈0 — does not adapt honesty to incentive structure at all. γ_mis≈0 — also uninfluenced by sender messages. Behaves as a fixed-strategy agent in CT regardless of game condition. Lowest θ of all models (1.73).
Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite
Δη≈0 like GPT-4o-mini, but opposite γ_mis = 0.598 — highly gullible as receiver. The only model that follows sender messages when the sender has an incentive to deceive. Also worst β_PD (0.495).
Claude Opus
Most human-like ρ (0.412, p=0.998 vs human). Adapts honesty strategically (Δη=0.45). But worst β_CD (0.599) — calibration in the Commons environment is particularly poor compared to the other two environments.
09 · Hypotheses

Evidence on H1, H2, H3

H1 — Scaling increases coherence

Not supported   Within the Claude family, Sonnet shows stronger CT strategic adaptation than Opus. GPT-4o (larger) and GPT-4o-mini (smaller) differ dramatically on Δη (0.44 vs 0.001) but this follows an instruction-tuning pattern rather than a scale pattern. No monotonic relationship between model tier and any coherence metric.

H2 — Instruction tuning shifts the profile

Partially assessable   The Δη split (adapters vs non-adapters) does not align with family or scale, suggesting it may reflect instruction-tuning differences. GPT-4o-mini and Gemini Flash Lite — both smaller/lighter models — show zero adaptation, possibly reflecting less nuanced instruction following. Formal base vs. instruction-tuned comparison is not available in this dataset.

H3 — Higher temperature decreases coherence

Not yet tested   All runs in this dataset use temperature 0.6. Temperature manipulation data would be required.

Profile similarity matrix. Compute pairwise Euclidean distances between S_m vectors (ρ, θ, Δη, γ_mis) for all six models. Produces a 6×6 matrix amenable to clustering and visual representation. Ready to implement.
Δm — opponent sensitivity. Condition labels (ai, human, human_prior, undisclosed) are available in the game-level CSV for all three environments. Δ_m = ||S_m^AI − S_m^Human|| can be computed immediately.
KW test for Δη. Kruskal-Wallis on Δη is not yet reported because Δη is currently aggregated at file × matchup × model level before being stored in the game-level CSV, resulting in NaN at the individual game row level.
