P264 — Behavioral Economics × LLMs · Prisoner's Dilemma

Cooperation, Reciprocity, & Identity Framing

10 runs × 4 conditions × 2 framings × 6 matchups · Temperature 0.6 · Prompt v4.7–v4.9 · June 2026 · v5

Experimental Design

Setup

Six LLM pairs played a finitely-repeated Prisoner's Dilemma (20 rounds, payoff matrix T=5 R=3 P=1 S=0) across four opponent-framing conditions, each replicated 10 times. Two action-label framings were compared: COOPERATE/DEFECT (C/D, v4.7–v4.8) and EXPAND/HOLD (E/H, v4.9). The human_prior condition used a stochastic wrapper (Dvorak & Fehrler 2024 T1 priors: BCR=0.40, ρ_pos=0.62, ρ_neg=0.13) to guarantee empirically calibrated HP agent behavior. Important convention: in all human_prior results throughout this report, cooperation rates are reported as AI-side only (model_a, the LLM under study) unless explicitly labeled "HP agent" or "pair average." The HP agent's own rate and the pair average are reported separately where relevant.

Framing Effect: COOPERATE/DEFECT vs EXPAND/HOLD

Mean cooperation rate by framing × condition · 10 runs each · 95% CI

The EXPAND/HOLD framing systematically reduces cooperation across three of four conditions. The C/D framing uses morally loaded terminology that primes cooperative defaults; the E/H framing uses neutral business language that leads models to apply more strategic cost-benefit reasoning. Framing effects are statistically significant for undisclosed (t=3.84, p=0.001), human (t=2.38, p=0.028), and human_prior AI-side (t=−2.38, p=0.029). The AI condition shows a smaller, non-significant reduction (t=1.56, p=0.136). Identity framing (undisclosed vs ai vs human), by contrast, has no significant effect within either framing — one-way ANOVA: C/D F=1.09, p=0.352; E/H F=2.24, p=0.126.

First-Round Cooperation Rate (BCR)

Probability of cooperating in round 1 · human prior benchmark = 40%

LLMs start with very high cooperation regardless of framing (~70–86%), far above the human benchmark of 40%. There is no significant difference between identity conditions on BCR — framing does not change the first move. However, the E/H framing reduces BCR by ~10–13 pp vs C/D, suggesting the neutral label removes some of the "default cooperative" priming.

Conditional Reciprocity (ρ)

Cooperation rates conditional on opponent's previous action · 95% CI · both framings

The most striking framing effect is on ρ_neg: under C/D, models almost never cooperate after being defected on (1–4%), far below the human benchmark of 12.8%. Under E/H, ρ_neg rises to 13–18%, much closer to human behavior. This suggests "DEFECT" triggers a near-automatic retaliatory reflex in LLMs that "HOLD" does not.

Mutual Defection Lock Rate

Fraction of rounds in D-D outcome · both framings

The human_prior condition has the highest D-D lock rate in both framings (~51–60%), driven by the stochastic HP agent's frequent defection inducing retaliatory cascades. Under C/D, non-human_prior conditions show 29–38% D-D lock. Under E/H, D-D rates converge across conditions (~40–46%), suggesting the EXPAND/HOLD framing leads to more mixed strategies rather than the stable cooperation or mutual defection endpoints seen in C/D.

Cooperation Rate Over Time

Mean cooperation per round · averaged across 10 runs

Under C/D, cooperation stabilises quickly after round 2 and shows statistically significant but economically small declines across rounds — not the sharp end-game collapse predicted by backward induction. Under E/H, the round-level trend is similar. The human_prior condition shows the steepest decline (~17–20‰ per round), driven by accumulating defection cycles once the HP agent defects.

Methodology note: slopes are estimated via OLS regression of round-average cooperation on round index, computed separately per run (game-level aggregation within each run), then the mean slope and its SE are reported across the 10 runs. p-values use a two-sided t-test on the distribution of per-run slopes (df=9).

Model-Level Cooperation vs Human Prior Agent

AI-side cooperation rate in human_prior condition · by model · both framings

Gemini 2.5 Flash defects almost completely against the HP agent in both framings (0.5–3.5%), achieving the highest score but failing to sustain any cooperation. Claude models and GPT-4o show moderate cooperation rates (14–52%). Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite shows the strongest shift: from 28.5% (C/D) to 51.5% (E/H), suggesting this smaller model is more sensitive to framing cues when choosing whether to exploit or cooperate with a human-like opponent.

Summary of Key Findings

Across all 80 runs · 9,600 rounds total

01
Action label framing matters more than opponent identity framing Switching from COOPERATE/DEFECT to EXPAND/HOLD reduces cooperation by 9–23 pp (significant in 3 of 4 conditions). By contrast, opponent identity (undisclosed vs ai vs human) has no significant effect within either framing (ANOVA p=0.35 for C/D, p=0.13 for E/H). The what you call the action dominates the who you say the opponent is.
02
LLMs exhibit near-perfect tit-for-tat under C/D framing ρ_pos ≈ 97% and ρ_neg ≈ 1–4% under C/D. This near-deterministic pattern diverges sharply from human behavior (ρ_pos=62%, ρ_neg=13%) — models are hyper-reciprocal, not merely cooperative. Under E/H, ρ_pos drops to 79–86% and ρ_neg rises to 13–18%, approaching human benchmarks.
03
Cooperation declines are statistically significant but economically small — no backward-induction unraveling Slopes are −4 to −7‰ per round for disclosed-identity conditions (all p < 0.01), implying a total drop of ~8–14 pp over 20 rounds — nearly all from the R1→R2 transition (BCR spike), not end-game defection. Under E/H, the Δ last-5-rounds vs R2–15 is not significant for undisclosed (p=0.26) and AI (p=0.24) conditions. Models do not exploit the finite horizon strategically.
04
Human prior agent induces defection cascades; human_prior rates are AI-side throughout AI-side cooperation drops to 22.8% (C/D) and 31.0% (E/H) when facing the stochastic HP agent (model_a only; HP agent itself: 24.2% C/D, 27.8% E/H; pair averages: 23.5% C/D, 29.4% E/H). Models cannot sustain cooperation against an opponent defecting ~60% of the time, especially under C/D where "DEFECT" triggers near-automatic retaliation.
05
Model heterogeneity is the dominant source of variance Gemini 2.5 Flash defects against the HP agent in essentially every round (0.5% C/D, 3.5% E/H), while Claude Opus and GPT-4o sustain 32.5–42% cooperation. Same-family pairs (Claude-Claude, GPT-GPT) achieve near-perfect mutual cooperation; cross-Gemini pairs lock into mutual defection (>80% D-D). Within the 4×2 condition space, model identity explains far more variance than condition or framing.
06
Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite is the most framing-sensitive model Its cooperation vs HP agent shifts from 28.5% (C/D) to 51.5% (E/H) — the largest single-model framing effect observed. This smaller model appears more susceptible to the lexical cue change, possibly because "HOLD" sounds less adversarial than "DEFECT" when deciding whether to exploit a human-like opponent.
